Introduction [to: Grace Notes Home Page]

Grace Notes never charges any fee or tuition for Bible study materials. The work is supported by Christians who pray for this effort and share in the expenses.

How it Works

You can register for one or more courses, as described below, we will send you the first lesson in the course. You can receive the course as e-mail text files, or as Microsoft Word attachments. The MS Word attachments are much easier to use, and they're formatted for printing. But "plain" e-mail is available, if you are not able to receive attachments.

Of special interest - you can use a course for teaching your own class! You can use the course for your own preparation, then you can print pages from the course for the class participants. You can use the built-in Quiz, or devise your own. The caveat here is that you must finish each lesson yourself before the next lesson will be sent. I won't be sending anyone the whole course in one mailing, rather ...

A course is sent to you one lesson at a time, and there is a Quiz with each lesson. The number of lessons in a course depends on the amount of material in it. The Ruth and Titus courses, for example, have ten (10) lessons each.

When you finish studying Lesson 1, for example, you will complete the Quiz (with a score of 70% or higher) and send it back to Grace Notes. Then, we will send you Lesson 2. (If your score is lower than 70%, you will receive a note asking you to retake the Quiz).  Note: every Quiz is an "open book" examination, meaning that you can find answers in your Bible, your notes, or the lessons themselves.  You are asked not to ask other people to help you with the answers to the Quiz.

This will continue until you have submitted the Quiz for the final lesson in the course.  When we receive your completed quiz for the last lesson of a course, we will send you a Certificate of Completion by regular mail.

The Extended Curriculum

Each course is part of a larger Unit of work. A Unit consists of several courses consisting of 15 to 18 credits of work. The amount of material in a Unit is roughly equivalent what you would find in one semester’s study at a Bible school.  As an example, the Grace Notes courses for Unit I are: Ruth (3 credits); Titus (3 credits); Foundations I (Equip) (2 credits); Doctrine I (3 credits); History I (2 credits); and an elective course (3 credits); for a total of 16 credits.

Units I to IV make up a  Bible Study Curriculum, which which leads to a Diploma of Biblical Studies.

A Certificate of Completion is awarded for each course you complete. 
Grace Notes will send you the certificate when you submit (and pass) the Quiz for the final lesson in the course.

An Award for Achievement will be awarded for the completion of a Unit.

A Diploma in Biblical Studies will be awarded to a person who completes Units I, II, III, and IV, equivalent to the amount of material in two years of Bible school study.

In a regular Bible School or college, a three-credit course requires about  150 hours of work to complete, which includes 54 hours in class (3 per week for 18 weeks) and 100 hours of personal study (roughly 2 hours study for each class hour).  I have tried to organize the Grace Notes courses so that they require about the same level of effort.

However, there is no time limit for completing Grace Notes courses. You can fit these studies into your own schedule.  Also, you may sign up for more than one course at a time. It is possible to finish Units I to IV in two years, or less, but this would require a lot of free time.

You can register for any available course (see the registration page), regardless of which Unit it is in. But you must finish all the courses in a Unit before being given credit for a higher unit. (Note the approximate dates when the courses will be available.)
